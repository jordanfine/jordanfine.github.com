Gather

I started working at Gather at the start of the design process of the marketplace application. Over the summer, I worked with the CTO and CEO to build out the marketplace from mockup to prototype to shipment. Beyond front-end development, I helped shape the design of Gather Marketplace by creating mockups, illustrations, email templates and redesigns for the existing restaurant software. I returned to Gather to continue my work on the marketplace in October.

MapFlix

MapFlix was my semester-long project from a Human Computer Interaction course. MapFlix is an application to help foreign film enthusiasts browse for films based off location using geographical visualization (Google Maps) versus the existing method used in Netflix. Throughout the semester, I identified a problem, interviewed potential clients of the proposed application, conducted focus groups, built the application and ran a pilot test study. After all the data was collected, I wrote user evaluations and analysed the data.

Though MapFlix wasn’t favored over Netflix, most users claimed they were moderately satisfied with the experience of Netflix. This gives room for designers to experiment with Netflix’s UX. The application was built using Google Maps and Freebase API.

Damage Assessment Portal

Damage Assessment Portal is an application for UF Admin to report any physical damages to its facilities during traumatic events. With the application, the University of Florida can keep a record of all damages for FEMA or any other appropriate government agency. One feature of the the application is the ability to view all of the damages on a map. For example, if a tornado blew across the north side of campus, we could visualize the damages and overlay it with the tornado's path. Before coding the application, I designed the UI in Photoshop. We built this application using PHP, Javascript and Twitter Bootstrap.

Fruitvale Station

This flier was designed for RUB Entertainment’s November Indie film, to be distributed across downtown Gainesville and on-campus. The flier’s design mirror’s a minimal representation of the BART rail system. After visiting the Bay Area over the summer and seeing the film, I was fascinated by the simplicity of the rails. I feel the simplicity of these rails juxtaposing the chaotic city reflects on the events that happened in Fruitvale Station -- a simple man trying to make his life better amongst the chaos of his past life. I’ll try not to spoil the movie; go see it!

RUB Promotional Video Montage

Back in 2012, I set out to modify and change the dynamics of RUB Entertainment’s graphic design operation. Over my four years in RUB, I’ve helped transform its branding and marketing.

During my freshman year, I became the main graphic designer for the Special Events committee and created fliers for monthly events. I noticed a major issue: Each committee had its own graphic designer creating different designs and nothing was consistent.

As Director of the Arts committee, I planned gallery openings and art-related events -- such as bringing Anthony Rapp from the broadway musical Rent. As director, I formed a group of designers to handle all of RUB’s graphics. Eventually, we created a Marketing Designer position. As a result, RUB as a brand is more cohesive and communication with directors who plan events has improved.

This video is an example of an idea I brought to the organization. This video played before every film at the Reitz Union Cinema and through online social-media during the month of September.
