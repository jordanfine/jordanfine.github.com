MapFlix

MapFlix was the semester long project from a Human Computer Interaction course. Throughout the semester we had to find a problem, interview potential clients of the proposed application, conduct focus groups, build the application, and run a pilot test study. After all the data was collected, we wrote user evaluations and analysed the data. MapFlix is an application to help foreign film enthusiasts browse for films based off location using geographical visualization (Google Maps) versus the existing method used in Netflix. Though MapFlix wasn’t favored over Netflix, most users were moderately satisfied with the experience of Netflix, which gives room for designers to experiment with Netflix’s UX. The application was built using Google Maps and Freebase API.

Damage Assessment Portal

The Damage Assessment Portal is an application for UF Admin to report any physical damages to their facilities during traumatic events. The purpose of the application is for the University of Florida to have a record of all damages for FEMA or any other government agencies that would need record of these damages. Other features in the application is to view all the damages on a map. For example, if a tornado went across the north side of campus, we could visualize the damages and overlay it with the tornado's path. Before coding the application, I designed the UI in Photoshop. We built this application using PHP, Javascript, and Twitter Bootstrap.

Fruitvale Station

This flyer was designed for RUB Entertainment’s November Indie film, to be distributed across downtown Gainesville and on-campus. The idea behind designing this flyer was to display a minimal representation of the BART rail system. After visiting the Bay Area over the summer and seeing the film, I was fascinated by the simplicity of the rails. I feel the simplicity of these rails juxtaposing the chaotic city reflects on the events that happened in Fruitvale Station, a simple man trying to make his life better amongst the chaos of his past life. RUB Promotional Video Montage

Back in 2012, I set out to modify and change the dynamics of the graphic design operation of RUB Entertainment. Over my four years in RUB, I’ve helped evolve the branding and marketing for the organization. Starting my freshman year, I joined the Promotions committee, which later became Special Events committee. On Special Events, I was the main graphic designer for the committee, creating flyers for our monthly events. I noticed a major issue, each committee had it’s own graphic designer creating completely different designs and nothing was consistent. Starting in 2012, I became Director of the Arts committee, planning gallery openings and art related events, such as bringing Anthony Rapp, from the broadway musical Rent. As Director of the Arts Committee, I formed a group of designers to handle all the graphics for the organization. When the year ended, we realized that the work was too much to handle for a director who must also plan events. That is how the Marketing Designer position on the Executive board was created. Working closely with the Vice President of Marketing, we have a more cohesive brand and communication with the director’s planning events. Over the course of my last year, I am planning on creating a branding guide for the organization to leave behind when I graduate.

This video is an example of an idea I brought to the organization. This video plays before every film at the Reitz Union Cinema and through online social-media during the month of September.
